## Key Findings Summary

### Hong Kong International Airport (VHHH)

**Operational Profile**
- 24/7 operations with NO curfews
- Modern 3-runway system (newly opened Nov 2024: 07L/25R, 07R/25L, 07C/25C)
- Each runway: 3,800 meters (12,467 feet)

**Risk Factors**
- **HIGH RISK**: Typhoon season (May-September) with established Flight Rescheduling Control System
- **HIGH RISK**: Slot-controlled with mandatory PPR for private/charter flights
- **HIGH RISK**: Stringent Hong Kong CAD regulations with 3-business-day minimum permit processing
- **MEDIUM RISK**: Airport operating near capacity (420,000 annual movements)
- **LOW RISK**: Modern infrastructure, 24/7 customs/immigration/health services

**Notable Requirements**
- Combined Single Limit (CSL) insurance mandatory with specific coverage requirements
- Landing permits mandatory for all operations
- Overflight permits required with Route Navigation Facility Charges
- Noise certificate required for subsonic jet aircraft (ICAO Annex 16 Chapter 3)

### Tokyo Narita International Airport (RJAA)

**Operational Profile**
- Restricted operating hours: 06:00-23:00 JST (21:00-14:00 UTC next day)
- Night ban: 23:00-06:00 JST (14:00-21:00 UTC) - NO OPERATIONS PERMITTED
- Two runways: 16R/34L (4,000m), 16L/34R (2,180m)
- Third runway under development (target: end of decade)

**Risk Factors**
- **CRITICAL**: Slot-controlled with mandatory coordination via Japan Schedule Coordination (JSC)
- **HIGH RISK**: Highly congested during peak seasons (June-Sept, holidays)
- **HIGH RISK**: Winter snow closures possible (Dec-Feb) with associated deicing delays
- **HIGH RISK**: Night curfew severely restricts operations
- **MEDIUM RISK**: Recently experienced significant disruptions (132 delays in Oct 2025)

**Notable Requirements**
- Slots must be applied for 15th of month prior for high-demand periods
- Mandatory Air Operator Certificate (AOC) and Certificate of Airworthiness (COA)
- Safety audit required for new cargo charter operators (30-40 days)
- General aviation parking: 14-19 spaces, max 30 days overnight (extendable in hangar)

### Miami Opa-locka Executive Airport (KOPF)

**Operational Profile**
- Operating hours: 09:00-24:00 ET (13:00-04:00 UTC)
- Control tower: 07:00-23:00 ET
- Three runways: 09L/27R (8,002 ft), 09R/27L (4,309 ft), 12/30 (6,800 ft)

**Risk Factors**
- **HIGH RISK**: Atlantic hurricane season (June-Nov, peak Aug-Oct)
- **MEDIUM RISK**: Tropical thunderstorms common (May-Oct)
- **MEDIUM RISK**: CBP international clearance required with 2-hour pre-departure notification
- **LOW RISK**: Reliever airport status means generally lower congestion
- **POSITIVE**: NO landing fees (unique advantage vs Miami International)

**Notable Requirements**
- CBP permission mandatory minimum 2 hours before departure from foreign location
- ETA/ETD tolerance: +/- 30 minutes (re-approval required if exceeded)
- Federal Inspection Services (FIS) for customs/immigration processing
- No inbound cargo permitted
- All aircraft must land at CBP ramp for inspection
