## KEY OPERATIONAL FINDINGS

### MUNICH (EDDM) - Germany's Premier International Hub

**Defining Characteristics:**
- Slot-controlled major hub with complex Alpine weather patterns
- Modern infrastructure with dual parallel runways (4000m each)
- Strict noise regulations with Bonus List system for night operations
- Significant seasonal demand volatility (ski season: Jan-Feb, summer: Jul-Aug)
- 40-50% traffic increase during ski season

**Critical Risk Factors:**
- **Weather:** Alpine proximity creates winter vulnerability; fog common Nov-Mar
- **Congestion:** Peak hours 10:00-18:00 with typical 5-15 min delays
- **Slots:** Level 2-3 coordination; PPR often required, 24+ hour advance booking
- **Noise:** Strict enforcement, Chapter 3 compliance mandatory, surcharges €500-2,000
- **Costs:** High-season fees €2,500-4,500 per movement

**Operational Advantage:**
Excellent infrastructure, modern A-CDM systems, reliable ground services, multiple quality alternates (Frankfurt, Stuttgart, Düsseldorf within 180-360 km)

---

### BIGGIN HILL (EGKB) - London's Dedicated Business Aviation Hub

**Defining Characteristics:**
- Single-runway business aviation airport near London
- Non-slot-controlled but noise-restricted (50,000 annual movement cap)
- London airspace coordination required (Class B airspace constraints)
- Excellent proximity to Central London (28 miles, ~51 minutes)
- Minimal weather disruption (South East England temperate climate)

**Critical Risk Factors:**
- **Runway:** Single 1806m runway limits large aircraft (max ~60-75 tonnes MTOW)
- **Noise:** Strict Noise Action Plan with Webtrak public monitoring system
- **Airspace:** London TMA coordination adds complexity; procedural approach (non-radar)
- **Weather:** Occasional fog (Nov-Mar) but minimal snow/ice
- **Hours:** Weekday 06:30-23:00, Weekend 08:00-22:00 (not 24/7)

**Operational Advantage:**
Closest business aviation airport to London, no slot requirements, 24/7 customs capability, low-congestion operations, premium FBO services, excellent alternates (London City 12.7km, Farnborough 28km, Heathrow 23.5km)

---

### VIENNA (LOWW) - Central Europe's Alpine Gateway

**Defining Characteristics:**
- Level 3 fully coordinated major hub, 24/7 operations
- Gateway to Alps creating extreme seasonal volatility
- Modern state-of-the-art infrastructure (Terminal 3 recently completed)
- Dual runways (3483m & 3600m) supporting parallel operations
- Winter deicing operations: 500+ staff, 94 specialized vehicles

**Critical Risk Factors:**
- **Ski Season Surge:** Extreme Jan-Feb congestion (40-60% traffic increase) - MOST SEVERE
- **Winter Deicing:** Required Oct-Apr; frequent ground delays (15-25 min when active)
- **Slots:** Level 3 coordination, peak capacity 68 movements/hour (05:00-18:55), severely constrained 21:00-06:55
- **Weather:** Alpine location creates fog, ice, snow patterns; CAT II/III capability essential
- **Costs:** High-season fees €2,800-4,200 per movement, deicing surcharges €500-2,000

**Operational Advantage:**
24/7 operations, dual modern runways, excellent winter infrastructure, strategic Central Europe location, reliable alternates (Graz 200km, Salzburg 280km, Prague 220km)

## CRITICAL OPERATIONAL INSIGHTS

### Extreme Risk Factor Findings

**Vienna's Ski Season Volatility (CRITICAL)**
- January-February: 40-60% traffic surge (most extreme of three airports)
- Direct impact: Slot unavailability, significant delays, surcharges
- Mitigation: Book slots 3+ months in advance, pre-arrange parking/hangarage
- Recommendation: Avoid February peak if flexibility possible; January more manageable

**Munich Winter Weather Risk (HIGH)**
- Alpine location creates fog (Nov-Mar), occasional snow
- CAT II/III capability strongly recommended for reliability
- Winter deicing adds 10-15 min ground delays when active
- Impact: 30-40% of winter flights experience minor delays

**Biggin Hill London Airspace Complexity (MODERATE)**

- Class B airspace requires London ATC coordination
- Single runway creates bottleneck during peak hours
- Impact: Moderate airspace delays possible despite uncongested airfield
